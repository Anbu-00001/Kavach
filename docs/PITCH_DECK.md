# Kavach — Pitch Deck (content + spoken script)

**Format:** 10 slides, ~3 minutes spoken. Each slide = one idea, large type, one visual. The script
is what you *say*; the bullets are what's *on the slide*. Designed for the live Round-2 panel and
re-usable as the demo-video voiceover.

**Design rules (these earn the Design/Presentation 25%):** one idea per slide · ≤ 12 words of text
on screen · the product (real phone) is the hero, not bullet points · dark slides, one accent color
per risk level (green / amber / red) reused from the app · end every slide landing on a single
sentence.

---

## Slide 1 — Title / hook
**On screen:** कवच **KAVACH** · "Stop fighting the voice. Fight the script." · *the offline scam-call shield*

**Say:** "This is Kavach — Sanskrit for *armor*. It's a scam-call shield that runs entirely on the
cheap phone the victim already owns. No cloud, no account — it doesn't even *have* permission to
touch the internet. Let me show you why that's lowkey the whole point."

## Slide 2 — The problem (make them feel it)
**On screen:** A photo of an older person on the phone. One number: **$12.5B** lost to fraud in 2024.

**Say:** "In 2024 Americans reported twelve and a half billion dollars lost to fraud. For people
over 60, the FBI puts the average loss at eighty-three thousand dollars — a life's savings, one
phone call. And AI voice cloning just made the call sound exactly like your grandson."

## Slide 3 — Why nothing protects *the victim*
**On screen:** three crossed-out icons — 📞 number filters · 🎙️ voice detectors · ☁️ cloud AI

**Say:** "Everything that exists protects banks and call centers. Number filters? Scammers rotate
numbers. Deepfake-voice detectors? Useless the second a real human reads the script — and they
break on phone audio. And all of them are cloud services that would have to stream Grandma's private
call to a server. Nobody is protecting the person actually on the call."

## Slide 4 — The insight (the whole pitch in one line)
**On screen:** **Voices change. The script never does.**

**Say:** "Okay here's the insight. The voice keeps getting more perfect — but the *manipulation*
never changes. Every scam, in every language, runs the same playbook: create urgency, demand
secrecy, ask for gift cards, impersonate authority, trigger panic. A flawless clone reading that
script still trips the pattern. So we just stopped analyzing the voice and started analyzing the
*intent*. That's the whole move."

## Slide 5 — What Kavach detects
**On screen:** the 8 tactic chips lighting up — URGENCY · SECRECY · GIFT CARDS · AUTHORITY ·
DISTRESS · ISOLATION · OTP PROBE · "IT'S ME" SPOOF

**Say:** "Kavach listens to the call on speaker and scores it against eight manipulation tactics in
real time. When the dangerous ones stack up — 'buy gift cards' *and* 'don't tell anyone' *and*
'right now' — it knows that combination is almost never anything but a scam."

## Slide 6 — LIVE DEMO (the heart — ~45s)
**On screen:** *(switch to the real phone, mirrored)* — and a small badge: **✈️ Airplane mode ON**

**Say + do:** "This is a real 4-gigabyte OPPO, and notice — airplane mode is on. Nothing can leave
this phone. I'll play a scam call." *(play the clip)* "Watch the banner. It hears the distress hook…
now the payment demand… and there — **HIGH, likely a scam**, with a plain-language reason and exactly
what to do: hang up and call back on a number you trust. All of that ran on the phone, offline."

> Fallback if live audio is risky: play the **pre-recorded screen capture** of this exact moment.
> Always have the recording ready.

## Slide 7 — The human circuit-breaker
**On screen:** **"What's our word?"** — the Watchword

**Say:** "Detection buys seconds; this is what saves the money. Families set a **Watchword** — a
secret word a voice clone can't know. When Kavach sees a relationship spoof, it prompts: *ask your
safe-word.* That one question ends the call."

## Slide 8 — Why on-device is the entire moat
**On screen:** four icons — 📵 private by architecture · 🧠 can't hallucinate · 📱 runs on $80 phone · ♾️ $0 to scale

**Say:** "Running on-device isn't a constraint we settled for — it's the whole advantage. It's
private by *architecture*: no internet permission, auditable in one line. The advice is pre-written,
so it can never hallucinate to a scared 80-year-old. It runs on the phone the victims actually own.
And with no servers, it scales to a *billion* phones at essentially zero cost. And it already speaks
twelve languages."

## Slide 9 — Real & honest
**On screen:** ✅ both layers validated on-device · "advisory, not a magic blocker"

**Say:** "This is real today — the live shield and a background guardian both run on that OPPO, and
we proved it on real voice. We're also honest about scope: Kavach is an early-warning *advisor* that
buys you time to think, with a human in the loop — not a black box that promises to block 100%."

## Slide 10 — Close
**On screen:** कवच — **"The shield for the phone you already own."** · contact / repo

**Say:** "Scammers have AI now. The people they target have a phone and basically no defense. Kavach
puts the defense *on* that phone — offline, private, and free to scale to everyone who needs it.
That's us picking the victim's side. Thank you."

## Delivery notes
- **Total ~3 min.** Slides 2–4 are the setup, Slide 6 is the payoff — don't rush the demo.
- Practice the demo until the HIGH verdict lands on cue. If the room is noisy, **use the recording.**
- Open *and* close on the same image (कवच + "the phone you already own") so it bookends.
- If asked one question, answer from [docs/AI_EVALUATION_PREP.md](AI_EVALUATION_PREP.md) — short,
  confident, honest. Never invent a metric.
